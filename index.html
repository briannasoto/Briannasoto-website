I am currently a second year student at the University of California, Riverside. My experience here at UCR has exposed me to the real world and has allowed me to grow as in individual both academically and personally. During my first year I worked with student employment services here on campus, this gave me a glimpse inside the field of Human Resources which is now something that I would like to pursue as a career choice. I think that studing sociology and the way we interact with one another will allow me to understand people better and in turn be more able to help them in any way that I can. Being a first year generation college student I have been able to experience first hand how difficult college life can be. During these years most students get their first jobs and oftentimes they are minimum wage jobs with little to no benefits. I know first hand how challenging new jobs can be and I would like to be someone that makes that process easier for employees. That is why my ultimate career goal is to become a Human Resource Manager. This position would allow me to be in charge of hiring and training processes for new employees. This interest me as I would like to be a source of support and aid to new employees. Also, I have had different jobs and know what it's like to be mistreated by those in higher positions than me. Working in Human Resources will allow me help those employees that feel that they are being mistreated by those above them and help solve whatever issues they may be facing in ther work environments.
